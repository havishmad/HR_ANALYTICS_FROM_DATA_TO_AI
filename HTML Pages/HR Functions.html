HR Functions & Capabilities Framework

A comprehensive overview of modern HR functional areas

Executive Summary

Human Resources departments have evolved from administrative personnel management to strategic business partners. This framework categorizes HR functions into core capabilities found in most organizations and specialized functions typically present in larger enterprises. Understanding this structure helps organizations assess their HR maturity and identify areas for strategic investment.

Core HR Functions (Operational Excellence)

Recruitment & Selection

Attracting, sourcing, screening, interviewing, and hiring employees.

Key Capabilities:

  • Workforce Planning
  • Job Analysis & Description
  • Sourcing
  • Screening
  • Interviewing
  • Testing & Assessments
  • Background Checks
  • Offer Negotiation & Onboarding
  • Employer Branding
  • DEI Recruiting
  • ATS Management

Compensation & Benefits

Designing and administering pay structures, benefits packages, and incentives.

Key Capabilities:

  • Salary Benchmarking
  • Job Evaluation
  • Payroll Administration
  • Benefits Design & Administration
  • Incentive Programs
  • Compensation Planning
  • Executive Compensation
  • Compliance
  • Benefits Communication

HR Administration & Compliance

Managing HR paperwork, records, and ensuring compliance with employment laws.

Key Capabilities:

  • HRIS Management
  • Recordkeeping & Documentation
  • Legal Compliance
  • Policy Development
  • Reporting & Analytics
  • Audits
  • Immigration Support
  • Data Privacy

Core HR Functions (Employee Experience)

Employee Relations

Managing the relationship between the organization and its employees, fostering a positive work environment.

Key Capabilities:

  • Conflict Resolution
  • Disciplinary Actions
  • Performance Management
  • Employee Engagement
  • Employee Surveys
  • Policy Enforcement
  • Investigations
  • Labor Relations
  • Wellness Programs
  • Internal Communications

Training & Development

Providing employees with the skills and knowledge they need to succeed.

Key Capabilities:

  • Needs Assessment
  • Training Program Design
  • Leadership Development
  • Performance Consulting
  • Succession Planning
  • LMS Administration
  • Training Evaluation
  • Career Development
  • Mentorship Programs

Specialized HR Functions (Strategic Value)

Talent Management

A holistic approach to attracting, developing, and retaining top talent (integrates many of the core functions).

Focuses on strategic workforce planning, succession planning, leadership development, and performance management at a high level.

HR Business Partner

A strategic HR role that works closely with business leaders to align HR strategies with business objectives. HRBPs act as consultants and advisors to specific departments or business units.

HR Analytics

Using data and analytics to make better HR decisions. This involves collecting, analyzing, and interpreting HR data to identify trends, predict outcomes, and improve HR processes.

Specialized HR Functions (Transformation)

Organizational Development

Focuses on improving organizational effectiveness and change management.

Key Capabilities:

  • Change Management
  • Organizational Design
  • Team Building
  • Culture Development
  • Process Improvement

Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion

Developing and implementing programs to promote a diverse, equitable, and inclusive workplace.

Employee Experience

Focused on creating a positive and engaging experience for employees throughout their entire journey with the company, from recruitment to offboarding.

Global Mobility

Managing the relocation of employees to different countries, including immigration, tax, and cultural considerations.

HR Function Maturity Model

Organizations typically evolve through several stages of HR maturity. Understanding your current position helps target strategic investments in HR capabilities.

1
Foundation
Basic compliance and administrative functions only
2
Developing
Emerging processes for core HR functions
3
Standardized
Consistent processes and technology across HR
4
Advanced
Data-driven HR aligned with business strategy
5
Optimized
HR as strategic business partner driving outcomes

Strategic Considerations for HR Leaders

Outsourcing

Many HR functions can be outsourced to third-party providers (e.g., payroll, benefits administration, recruiting) to improve efficiency and reduce costs.

Technology

HR technology (HRIS, ATS, LMS, performance management systems) plays a crucial role in managing HR processes and data, enabling greater efficiency and insights.

Industry-Specific Needs

Certain industries may have unique HR needs (e.g., healthcare, manufacturing, technology) that require specialized approaches or compliance considerations.

Company Size

Small businesses may combine many functions into a single HR generalist role, while large corporations will have dedicated teams for each subfunction.
