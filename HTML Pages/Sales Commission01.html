Sales Commission Structures

Strategic approaches to incentivize and reward sales performance

1

Fixed Commission Per Sale

Simple, predictable, volume-focused

How it Works: Salespeople earn a fixed amount per unit sold, regardless of the value.

Pros
  • Simple and easy to understand
  • Encourages volume-based selling
  • Predictable commission costs
Cons
  • Doesn't incentivize higher-value deals
  • Can lead to a focus on low-margin products
2

Tiered Commission Structure

Progressive, achievement-based

How it Works: Different commission rates apply as salespeople reach higher targets (e.g., 5% up to ₹10L, 7% for ₹10L-₹20L, 10% above ₹20L).

Pros
  • Encourages overperformance
  • Rewards top performers without overpaying low performers
  • Keeps motivation high throughout the month/quarter
Cons
  • Complexity in calculation and tracking
  • Salespeople might hold deals to maximize commission in the next period
3

Profit-Based Commission

Margin-focused, profitability-aligned

How it Works: Commission is based on the profit margin of each sale rather than revenue.

Pros
  • Aligns sales incentives with company profitability
  • Encourages selling higher-margin products/services
Cons
  • Can demotivate sales teams if margin control is outside their influence
  • Harder to calculate, requires detailed financial tracking
4

Team-Based Commission

Collaborative, group-oriented

How it Works: Commission is distributed based on the overall performance of a team rather than individual sales.

Pros
  • Encourages teamwork and knowledge sharing
  • Can improve overall sales consistency
Cons
  • High performers may feel demotivated if others don't contribute equally
  • Can lead to internal conflicts over effort distribution
5

Hybrid Model (Base + Commission)

Balanced, security with incentive

How it Works: A fixed salary with a commission component based on sales performance.

Pros
  • Ensures income stability while keeping incentives for performance
  • Attracts experienced sales professionals who seek job security
Cons
  • Reduces the direct link between performance and earnings
  • Can be costly if commissions are not structured properly
6

Commission with Accelerators

High-powered, performance-driven

How it Works: Higher commission rates apply once a certain threshold is exceeded (e.g., 5% up to ₹10L, 15% after ₹20L).

Pros
  • Highly motivating for top performers
  • Drives aggressive sales behavior
Cons
  • Complex structure for payroll calculations
  • Can lead to salespeople delaying deals to cross into higher tiers
7

Customer Retention-Based Commission

Relationship-focused, long-term oriented

How it Works: Salespeople earn commission based on client retention or repeat purchases.

Pros
  • Encourages long-term relationships rather than one-time sales
  • Reduces churn and improves customer lifetime value
Cons
  • Payouts are delayed, which may demotivate some salespeople
  • May not work in industries with one-time sales cycles
8

Milestone-Based Commission

Process-oriented, staged incentives

How it Works: Commission is earned at different stages of the sales process (e.g., lead qualification, demo completion, contract signing).

Pros
  • Motivates salespeople throughout the sales cycle
  • Reduces risk of deals getting stuck at late stages
Cons
  • Complicated to track and administer
  • Requires clear definitions for each milestone
9

Weighted Product/Service Commission

Strategic, priority-focused

How it Works: Higher commission rates for strategic or high-priority products (e.g., 10% on new products, 5% on old ones).

Pros
  • Aligns sales efforts with business goals
  • Encourages adoption of new or high-margin offerings
Cons
  • May divert attention from other important products
  • Requires frequent adjustments to commission structure
10

Salary with Quarterly Bonus

Stable, goal-oriented

How it Works: Fixed salary with a quarterly bonus based on achieving pre-set KPIs.

Pros
  • Provides income stability
  • Encourages long-term goal setting rather than short-term push
Cons
  • Less immediate motivation compared to commission-based models
  • Can lead to complacency if not structured well

Recommendation

The best model depends on your industry, sales cycle, and business goals. If short-term revenue growth is the focus, a tiered or accelerator model works well. If customer retention is key, a retention-based or profit-based commission is better. You can also combine models to balance stability and motivation.
