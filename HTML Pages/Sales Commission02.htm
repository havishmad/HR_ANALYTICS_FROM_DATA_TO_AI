Sales Compensation Strategies

Comparative Analysis of Different Approaches

Tiered Commission Structure

Pros

  • Motivates high performers to exceed targets
  • Rewards incremental growth at different levels
  • Creates clear performance milestones

Cons

  • May create a "sandbagging" effect if reps slow down after hitting a tier
  • More complex to administer
Medium Complexity Individual Focus Short-term

Product Mix/Category-Based Commission

Pros

  • Encourages focus on strategic or higher-margin products
  • Aligns sales force efforts with company priorities

Cons

  • May reduce focus on lower-margin products (even if they are essential)
  • Requires consistent tracking of different product categories
Medium Complexity Individual Focus Medium-term

Profitability-Focused Commission

Pros

  • Drives sales of higher-margin deals
  • Aligns rep incentives closely with profitability goals

Cons

  • Complex to implement if cost/margin data is not readily available
  • Reps may ignore lower-profit but strategically important deals
High Complexity Individual Focus Medium-term

Commission for Cross-Selling/Upselling

Pros

  • Boosts average deal size
  • Builds deeper customer relationships
  • Encourages reps to fully understand the product suite

Cons

  • Can lead to overly pushy sales tactics that negatively impact customer satisfaction
  • May detract from quick, straightforward sales if not balanced
Medium Complexity Individual Focus Short-term

Team-Based or Pool Commission

Pros

  • Encourages collaboration and knowledge sharing
  • Rewards overall team performance

Cons

  • High performers may feel under-rewarded if others underperform
  • Hard to measure individual contributions within a team
Medium Complexity Team Focus Medium-term

Recurring Revenue/Retention Commission

Pros

  • Incentivizes long-term customer satisfaction and renewals
  • Stabilizes income for both company and reps

Cons

  • Might dilute focus on immediate new sales
  • Complicated to track if renewals depend on multiple factors outside the rep's control
High Complexity Individual Focus Long-term

Activity-Based Bonuses

Pros

  • Reinforces important sales behaviors (e.g., demos, calls, follow-ups)
  • Helps build consistent sales pipelines

Cons

  • Activity without results can be costly
  • Requires careful definition of valuable activities and measurement tools
Low Complexity Individual Focus Short-term

Accelerated Monthly/Quarterly Commission

Pros

  • Spurs earlier and more intense effort
  • Motivates reps to stay consistently ahead of targets

Cons

  • May lead to short-term spike in activity and potential "sales drop" in subsequent periods
  • Can complicate commission calculations and forecasting
Medium Complexity Individual Focus Short-term

Hybrid Base + Variable Pay

Pros

  • Offers income stability while maintaining performance incentives
  • Can use multipliers for specific metrics (customer satisfaction, short sales cycle, etc.)

Cons

  • Might create a sense of entitlement to the base pay
  • Balancing the base vs. variable portion can be tricky
Low Complexity Individual Focus Medium-term

Milestone-Based Bonuses

Pros

  • Drives specific strategic goals (new market entry, target accounts, product launches)
  • Allows for flexibility in shifting priorities

Cons

  • May be harder to define or track certain milestones
  • Could distract from core revenue-generating activities if milestones are overly specific or too numerous
Medium Complexity Individual Focus Medium-term
