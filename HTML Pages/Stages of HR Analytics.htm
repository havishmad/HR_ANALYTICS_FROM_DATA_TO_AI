The Four Stages of HR Analytics

A progression from reporting to strategic decision-making

1

Descriptive Analytics

What happened?

Examples

  • Employee turnover rates
  • Absenteeism patterns
  • Compensation distribution
  • Demographic breakdowns

HR Applications

Creating dashboards showing headcount trends, monitoring diversity metrics, or tracking recruitment funnel conversion rates

2

Diagnostic Analytics

Why did it happen?

Examples

  • Correlation between engagement scores and turnover
  • Identifying factors that impact time-to-hire
  • Performance drivers analysis
  • Compensation equity studies

HR Applications

Determining why certain departments experience higher turnover, understanding what drives employee satisfaction scores

3

Predictive Analytics

What will happen?

Examples

  • Flight risk modeling
  • Forecasting future talent needs
  • Predicting candidates likely to succeed
  • Performance trajectory modeling

HR Applications

Creating early warning systems for potential turnover, predicting which employees might be ready for promotion

4

Prescriptive Analytics

What should we do?

Examples

  • Personalized development recommendations
  • Optimized compensation modeling
  • AI-powered workforce planning
  • Intervention effectiveness simulation

HR Applications

Suggesting targeted retention strategies for at-risk employees, recommending optimal interview panel composition for different roles
